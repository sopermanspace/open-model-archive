### Nimbus Relay Secures $45M Seed Round to Pioneer ‘Latency Insurance’ for Critical APIs

**SAN FRANCISCO – March 14, 2027 –** Nimbus Relay, a startup focused on API reliability and performance guarantees, announced today that it has closed a $45 million seed funding round. The investment, led by Signal VC and angel investors from Stripe, will fuel the expansion of its groundbreaking platform designed to provide contractual "latency insurance" for mission-critical API calls.

Nimbus Relay fundamentally changes how businesses measure uptime. Instead of traditional uptime percentages, the service guarantees performance at specific percentile thresholds. If a client's 99th percentile latency (p99) exceeds a negotiated Service Level Agreement (SLA) threshold—say, above 300ms during peak usage hours—the customer receives automatic, measurable credits against their monthly billing.

The platform achieves this guarantee using proprietary AI models that continuously monitor network jitter and predict degradation points, allowing clients to shift from reactive monitoring to proactive risk mitigation. According to the technical whitepaper released alongside the funding announcement, Nimbus Relay’s predictive engine uses a novel Bayesian queuing model, dramatically reducing false positive alerts compared to standard APM tools.

“Traditional observability simply tells you *when* something broke; we tell you if it will break and quantify exactly what that failure costs,” said CEO Elena Rodriguez. “Our clients run hyper-scale services—from financial trading platforms to global e-commerce—where a millisecond delay means millions in lost revenue. We are finally moving from hoping for uptime to guaranteeing performance.”

The market reaction has been immediate. While major players like Datadog and Dynatrace have historically focused on monitoring, competitors now face mounting pressure to offer similar risk-based guarantees. One notable comment came from CloudGuard Solutions, which stated they "will need a rapid product feature expansion or face obsolescence in the reliability space."

Early adopters are already praising the system's transparency. “Before Nimbus Relay, latency was an accepted operational risk,” said Marcus Chen, CTO of Ascent Fintech. “Now it’s an auditable variable on our P&L statement. It is transformative.”

The capital raised will be used to expand enterprise sales teams and integrate new monitoring verticals, including low-earth orbit satellite communication links for highly distributed clients.

**About Nimbus Relay**
Nimbus Relay provides performance-as-a-service (PaaS) through its industry-first latency insurance framework. By quantifying API slowdowns at the p99 percentile and automatically issuing credits upon SLA breaches, Nimbus ensures that high-stakes digital services maintain predictable speed, safeguarding revenue streams for the world's most demanding enterprises.
