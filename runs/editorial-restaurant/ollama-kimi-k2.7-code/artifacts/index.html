The chef's story

Margot Vance grew up splitting firewood on her grandfather’s farm in the Willamette Valley, where dinner was whatever the orchard, garden, and larder allowed. That sense of restraint—and reverence—still shapes every plate at Ember & Oak.

After a decade in kitchens from Copenhagen to the Sonoma coast, Margot returned to Oregon with a simple conviction: the finest dining is also the most honest. Here, whole grains are milled nearby, produce arrives before sunrise from farms she knows by name, and proteins are cooked over a hand-tended oak fire that has not gone out since we opened.

The long tables in our dining room were built from a single fallen Douglas fir. They are communal by design, because a good evening is measured less by courses than by the conversations that unfold between them.

A sample evening

Our menu changes with the season and the harvest. Below is one possible path through a recent evening.

  1. First

    Charred leek & rye

    Young leeks blackened over embers, paired with fermented rye crema, toasted seeds, and a soft-cooked farm egg.

  2. Main

    Oak-fired lamb with wild grains

    Shoulder from a Portland-area ranch, slow-roasted over white oak, served with emmer, nettle, and preserved spring onion.

  3. Dessert

    Hazelnut ash & honey

    Toasted Willamette hazelnuts, barely sweetened mascarpone, and raw honeycomb finished with a whisper of smoked salt.
