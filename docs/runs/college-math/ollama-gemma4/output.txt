## Solution to College-Level Math Problems

### 1. Calculus — Find the derivative of $f(x) = x^3 \cdot \ln(x)$ for $x > 0$.

We must use the Product Rule, which states that if $f(x) = u(x)v(x)$, then $f'(x) = u'(x)v(x) + u(x)v'(x)$.

Let:
$u(x) = x^3$
$v(x) = \ln(x)$

Calculate the derivatives of $u$ and $v$:
$u'(x) = 3x^2$
$v'(x) = \frac{1}{x}$

Apply the Product Rule:
$$f'(x) = u'(x)v(x) + u(x)v'(x)$$
$$f'(x) = (3x^2)(\ln(x)) + (x^3)\left(\frac{1}{x}\right)$$
$$f'(x) = 3x^2 \ln(x) + x^2$$

**Final Answer:**
The derivative is $f'(x) = 3x^2 \ln(x) + x^2$.

### 2. Calculus — Evaluate the definite integral $\int_0^1 (2x + 1) dx$.

We use the Fundamental Theorem of Calculus, finding the antiderivative $F(x)$ and evaluating $F(1) - F(0)$.

**Step 1: Find the indefinite integral (Antiderivative).**
$$\int (2x + 1) dx = \int 2x \, dx + \int 1 \, dx$$
$$\int (2x + 1) dx = 2\left(\frac{x^2}{2}\right) + x + C$$
$$F(x) = x^2 + x$$

**Step 2: Evaluate the integral using the limits of integration.**
$$\int_0^1 (2x + 1) dx = [x^2 + x]_0^1$$
$$\text{Evaluation} = F(1) - F(0)$$
$$\text{Evaluation} = ((1)^2 + 1) - ((0)^2 + 0)$$
$$\text{Evaluation} = (1 + 1) - (0)$$
$$\text{Evaluation} = 2$$

**Final Answer:**
The value of the definite integral is $2$.

### 3. Linear algebra — Given matrix $A = \begin{pmatrix} 2 & 1 \\ 1 & 3 \end{pmatrix}$, compute $A^{-1}$ if it exists.

For a $2 \times 2$ matrix $A = \begin{pmatrix} a & b \\ c & d \end{pmatrix}$, the determinant is $\det(A) = ad - bc$. If $\det(A) \neq 0$, the inverse is:
$$A^{-1} = \frac{1}{\det(A)} \begin{pmatrix} d & -b \\ -c & a \end{pmatrix}$$

**Step 1: Compute the determinant.**
$$\det(A) = (2)(3) - (1)(1)$$
$$\det(A) = 6 - 1$$
$$\det(A) = 5$$
Since $\det(A) = 5 \neq 0$, the inverse exists.

**Step 2: Compute the inverse matrix.**
$$A^{-1} = \frac{1}{5} \begin{pmatrix} 3 & -1 \\ -1 & 2 \end{pmatrix}$$
$$A^{-1} = \begin{pmatrix} 3/5 & -1/5 \\ -1/5 & 2/5 \end{pmatrix}$$

**Final Answer:**
The inverse matrix is $A^{-1} = \begin{pmatrix} 3/5 & -1/5 \\ -1/5 & 2/5 \end{pmatrix}$.

### 4. Linear algebra — Solve the system:
$$2x + y = 7 \quad (Eq 1)$$
$$x - 3y = -1 \quad (Eq 2)$$

We will use the elimination method. Our goal is to eliminate one variable, $x$.

**Step 1: Multiply Eq 2 by $-2$ and add it to Eq 1.**
Multiply $(Eq 2)$ by $-2$:
$$-2(x - 3y) = -2(-1)$$
$$-2x + 6y = 2 \quad (Eq 3)$$

Add $(Eq 1)$ and $(Eq 3)$:
$$(2x + y) + (-2x + 6y) = 7 + 2$$
$$7y = 9$$
$$y = \frac{9}{7}$$

**Step 2: Substitute the value of $y$ back into Eq 1 to find $x$.**
$$2x + y = 7$$
$$2x + \frac{9}{7} = 7$$

Isolate $2x$:
$$2x = 7 - \frac{9}{7}$$

Find a common denominator for the right side ($7 = 49/7$):
$$2x = \frac{49}{7} - \frac{9}{7}$$
$$2x = \frac{40}{7}$$

Solve for $x$:
$$x = \frac{40}{14}$$
$$x = \frac{20}{7}$$

**Final Answer:**
The solution to the system is $(x, y) = \left(\frac{20}{7}, \frac{9}{7}\right)$.

### 5. Probability — A fair coin is flipped 5 times. What is the probability of exactly 3 heads?

This is a binomial probability problem.
The formula is: $P(X=k) = \binom{n}{k} p^k (1-p)^{n-k}$

Where:
*   $n = 5$ (Number of trials/flips)
*   $k = 3$ (Desired number of successes/heads)
*   $p = 0.5$ (Probability of success, since the coin is fair)
*   $(1-p) = 0.5$ (Probability of failure)

**Step 1: Calculate the number of ways to get 3 heads in 5 flips ($\binom{5}{3}$).**
$$\binom{5}{3} = \frac{5!}{3!(5-3)!} = \frac{5!}{3!2!} = \frac{5 \times 4}{2 \times 1} = 10$$

**Step 2: Calculate the probability for this specific case.**
The probability of getting $k$ successes and $(n-k)$ failures is $(0.5)^3 (0.5)^{5-3} = (0.5)^3 (0.5)^2$.
Since the bases are the same, we add the exponents:
$$(0.5)^{3+2} = (0.5)^5$$

**Step 3: Calculate the final probability.**
$$P(X=3) = \binom{5}{3} \cdot (0.5)^5$$
$$P(X=3) = 10 \cdot \left(\frac{1}{2}\right)^5$$
$$P(X=3) = 10 \cdot \frac{1}{32}$$
$$P(X=3) = \frac{10}{32} = \frac{5}{16}$$

**Final Answer:**
The probability of exactly 3 heads is $\frac{5}{16}$ (or $0.3125$).
