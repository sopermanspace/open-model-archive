Do NOT let these socks near your ankles
By Brad K. - Denver, CO
Verified Purchase
Reviewed on March 14, 2024

I bought the SmartSock Pro X on February 2nd after three nights of bad sleep and one too many podcasts promising "biometric foot computing." What arrived was a single pair of gray socks in a box big enough for a microwave, padded with what looked like shredded tax forms. The shipping label said "Fragile: Circadian Electronics," but the socks inside had one toe already fraying.

The setup process is nonsense. You pair each sock via Bluetooth, name them "Luna" and "Sol" in the app, then calibrate by walking in a circle at "dawn-simulating brightness." My downstairs neighbor now thinks I belong to a cult.

The absurd feature bullet that sold me was the "Midnight Lunar Compression Loom," which supposedly knits micro-currents into your arches to "recalibrate melatonin geometry." In reality it's just a heating pad that turns on at 3 a.m. and makes your feet smell like a warm laptop. The "ankle telemetry" notifications kept vibrating to tell me my left ankle was "92% nocturnally misaligned," whatever that means. I turned off all push alerts and it texted my emergency contact instead.

After six nights my sleep score somehow went down twelve points and I got a mild rash. I contacted support and they asked if I were "hydrating with moon-charged water." I returned them. My regular cotton socks are undefeated.

Seller response:
Hi Brad, thank you for aligning with SmartSock Pro X. The emergency contact alert is a premium feature and indicates your ankle telemetry is working as intended. Please try our companion app update, "Circadian Socks 2.0: The Awakening," and remember that discomfort is simply your chronotype being disrupted into greatness.
