1.0 out of 5 stars
Literal shock treatment disguised as wellness. Do not buy!
By Gary H. (Des Moines, IA) on March 14, 2026
Verified Purchase

I was genuinely hoping the SmartSock Pro X would help with my chronic insomnia and jet lag, but this product has been an absolute nightmare from start to finish. First of all, the shipping was a complete disaster. I paid an extra $15 for expedited two-day delivery, but it took nearly three weeks to arrive, and the shipping box looked like it had been dragged behind the delivery truck. 

When I finally got them, I downloaded the buggy app and put the socks on before bed. The company claims these will "optimize circadian alignment via ankle telemetry," which apparently is just a fancy way of saying the socks vibrate aggressively at random intervals. But the absolute worst part is the "REM-Cycle Galvanic Pulse" feature. It is advertised as a gentle nervous system stimulator, but it feels exactly like getting zapped by a static shock from a metal doorknob, right on your sensitive ankle bone, every forty-five minutes. My wife actually kicked me out of the bedroom on night two because I kept jolting awake and screaming.

On top of all that, they are hand-wash only and take two full days to air dry because of the bulky battery pack embedded in the heel. I tried to return them, but customer service said the warranty is void once they have touched human skin. Save your money and just drink some warm milk.

Title: Great for nothing but damp ankles. Do not buy the Pro X.

Reviewed in Seattle, WA on October 12, 2023
Verified Purchase

I am genuinely disappointed with these SmartSock Pro Xs. The hype around "circadian alignment" is just ridiculous; they clearly haven't tested these socks on any actual humans who just want to walk to the coffee shop without having their basic biomechanics monitored. They feel exactly like wearing damp athletic socks, but for reasons I cannot explain (and definitely not biological ones).

First off, the shipping was a nightmare—they arrived wrapped in what looked suspiciously like industrial cling wrap instead of proper packaging, and one of the ankle sensors was bent. The instruction manual is also bafflingly complex; it required me to download an app that then demanded my blood type and my emotional state before acknowledging basic sock functionality.

And let's not even talk about the promised features. They listed "Optimal heel pressure for subconscious dream interpretation," which sounds less like medical technology and more like something conjured up during a bad ayahuasca trip. The battery life is atrocious, too—I had to charge them three times in one day just trying to keep the ambient light sensor happy.

The seller did send a generic reply stating that I should ensure my 'lifestyle congruence' was optimized, which I plan to disregard immediately. Stick to plain cotton socks; they are vastly more dependable. Total waste of money.

★ 1.0 out of 5 stars
Do NOT let these socks near your ankles
By Brad K. - Denver, CO
Verified Purchase
Reviewed on March 14, 2024

I bought the SmartSock Pro X on February 2nd after three nights of bad sleep and one too many podcasts promising "biometric foot computing." What arrived was a single pair of gray socks in a box big enough for a microwave, padded with what looked like shredded tax forms. The shipping label said "Fragile: Circadian Electronics," but the socks inside had one toe already fraying.

The setup process is nonsense. You pair each sock via Bluetooth, name them "Luna" and "Sol" in the app, then calibrate by walking in a circle at "dawn-simulating brightness." My downstairs neighbor now thinks I belong to a cult.

The absurd feature bullet that sold me was the "Midnight Lunar Compression Loom," which supposedly knits micro-currents into your arches to "recalibrate melatonin geometry." In reality it's just a heating pad that turns on at 3 a.m. and makes your feet smell like a warm laptop. The "ankle telemetry" notifications kept vibrating to tell me my left ankle was "92% nocturnally misaligned," whatever that means. I turned off all push alerts and it texted my emergency contact instead.

After six nights my sleep score somehow went down twelve points and I got a mild rash. I contacted support and they asked if I were "hydrating with moon-charged water." I returned them. My regular cotton socks are undefeated.

Seller response:
Hi Brad, thank you for aligning with SmartSock Pro X. The emergency contact alert is a premium feature and indicates your ankle telemetry is working as intended. Please try our companion app update, "Circadian Socks 2.0: The Awakening," and remember that discomfort is simply your chronotype being disrupted into greatness.
