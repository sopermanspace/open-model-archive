## Puzzle

A farmer must cross a river with a wolf, a goat, and a cabbage. The boat holds only the farmer plus one item. If left alone, the wolf eats the goat, and the goat eats the cabbage.

## Requirements

1. State the goal clearly.
2. List valid moves step by step (numbered).
3. Explain why each move is necessary — no skipped steps.
4. Confirm all four arrive safely on the far bank.
5. If you identify any impossible variant, say so — but this puzzle is solvable.

Output plain text. Use clear headings with `##` if helpful. No code.

## Goal

The goal is to safely transport the farmer, the wolf, the goat, and the cabbage from the starting bank of the river to the far bank, using a boat that can only carry the farmer and one other item at a time. The wolf and goat cannot be left alone together on either bank, nor can the goat and cabbage.

## Step-by-Step Solution

1. **The farmer takes the goat across to the far bank.**
   *Reasoning:* If the farmer takes the wolf first, the goat will eat the cabbage. If the farmer takes the cabbage first, the wolf will eat the goat. The goat is the common factor in both conflict scenarios, so it must be removed from the starting bank first.

2. **The farmer returns alone to the starting bank.**
   *Reasoning:* The farmer needs to bring the boat back to pick up the remaining items (the wolf and the cabbage), leaving the goat safely alone on the far bank.

3. **The farmer takes the wolf across to the far bank.**
   *Reasoning:* The farmer must transport one of the remaining items. Bringing the wolf is a valid next step (bringing the cabbage here is also a valid alternative path).

4. **The farmer returns to the starting bank with the goat.**
   *Reasoning:* If the farmer leaves the wolf and the goat on the far bank to go back for the cabbage, the wolf will eat the goat. Therefore, the farmer must take the goat back to the starting bank to prevent this.

5. **The farmer leaves the goat on the starting bank and takes the cabbage across to the far bank.**
   *Reasoning:* The farmer drops off the goat (which is now separated from the cabbage) and takes the cabbage across to join the wolf. The wolf and cabbage are safe to be left together.

6. **The farmer returns alone to the starting bank.**
   *Reasoning:* The farmer leaves the wolf and cabbage safely on the far bank and goes back to pick up the last remaining item, the goat.

7. **The farmer takes the goat across to the far bank.**
   *Reasoning:* This is the final step to transport the last item across the river.

## Conclusion

By following these steps, the farmer successfully transports the wolf, the goat, and the cabbage across the river. All four arrive safely on the far bank without any of the animals eating the others' food or each other.

## Goal

Get the farmer, the wolf, the goat, and the cabbage across the river without ever leaving the wolf alone with the goat, or the goat alone with the cabbage. The boat carries only the farmer plus one item.

## Why the first move matters

If the farmer starts by taking the **wolf**, the goat and cabbage are left together and the goat eats the cabbage.  
If the farmer starts by taking the **cabbage**, the wolf and goat are left together and the wolf eats the goat.  
So the first crossing must be with the **goat**, separating the two pairs.

## Step-by-step solution

1. **Farmer takes the goat across the river.**  
   - Near bank: wolf, cabbage.  
   - Far bank: farmer, goat.  
   - Reason: Only safe first move. Wolf and cabbage can be left together safely.

2. **Farmer returns alone.**  
   - Near bank: farmer, wolf, cabbage.  
   - Far bank: goat.  
   - Reason: The farmer must go back to fetch another item.

3. **Farmer takes the wolf across.**  
   - Near bank: cabbage.  
   - Far bank: farmer, wolf, goat.  
   - Reason: Moves the wolf. Now the goat is with the farmer and wolf on the far bank, so it is safe.

4. **Farmer brings the goat back.**  
   - Near bank: farmer, goat, cabbage.  
   - Far bank: wolf.  
   - Reason: If the farmer left the goat with the wolf and returned alone, the wolf would eat the goat. The goat must come back.

5. **Farmer takes the cabbage across.**  
   - Near bank: goat.  
   - Far bank: farmer, wolf, cabbage.  
   - Reason: Moves the cabbage. The wolf and cabbage can be left together safely.

6. **Farmer returns alone.**  
   - Near bank: farmer, goat.  
   - Far bank: wolf, cabbage.  
   - Reason: The farmer must fetch the last item.

7. **Farmer takes the goat across.**  
   - Near bank: empty.  
   - Far bank: farmer, wolf, goat, cabbage.  
   - Reason: Brings the goat to the far bank. The farmer is present, so nothing is eaten.

## Confirmation

All four — farmer, wolf, goat, and cabbage — arrive safely on the far bank. At no point was the wolf left alone with the goat, nor the goat left alone with the cabbage.

## Note on impossible variants

A variant where the boat cannot carry the goat back from the far bank (for example, if the farmer is not allowed to bring anything back) would be impossible, because the wolf and goat can never be left together. The standard puzzle is solvable as shown above.
